Uranus

The Ice Giant with a Tilted Spin

Overview

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and has a unique rotational axis tilted at over 90 degrees. This causes extreme seasonal variations and a rolling orbit unlike any other planet in the solar system.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~2.87 billion km
  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Surface Gravity: ~0.89 g
  • Day Length: ~17 hours
  • Year Length: 84 Earth years
  • Temperature: ~−224 °C (coldest of all planets)
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, methane

Structure & Composition

Uranus is composed mainly of water, ammonia, and methane ices above a rocky core. Its pale blue hue is due to methane gas absorbing red light. The planet also contains layered cloud decks and faint rings.

Orbital & Axial Tilt

Its extreme axial tilt (~98°) likely resulted from a massive collision early in its history. This causes it to spin on its side and has profound effects on its climate and ring orientation.

Exploration History

Uranus has only been visited by one spacecraft—Voyager 2 in 1986. The flyby revealed its ring system, 27 moons, and a surprisingly featureless atmosphere compared to other gas giants.

Why It’s Unique

Uranus stands out for its axial tilt, cold atmosphere, and methane-rich clouds. It's also the only planet that essentially orbits the Sun "on its side."
