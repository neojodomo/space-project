Mars

The Red Planet

Overview

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known for its striking red appearance caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It has the most Earth-like climate of any planet and has long intrigued scientists as a possible home for past or present life.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~228 million km
  • Diameter: 6,792 km
  • Surface Gravity: ~0.38 g
  • Day Length: ~24.6 hours
  • Year Length: ~687 Earth days
  • Average Temperature: −63°C
  • Atmosphere: Mostly CO₂, thin atmosphere

Surface & Geography

Mars features the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and a massive canyon system, Valles Marineris. Its surface includes plains, polar ice caps, ancient riverbeds, and dust storms.

Moons

Mars has two small, irregular moons: Phobos and Deimos. They are thought to be captured asteroids from the asteroid belt.

Exploration

Mars has been extensively explored by orbiters, landers, and rovers. Missions like Viking, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, Perseverance, and Ingenuity have provided valuable insights into its geology and potential for life.

Potential for Life

Although no life has been discovered on Mars yet, evidence of liquid water in the past raises hope. Future missions aim to return samples and pave the way for potential human exploration.
