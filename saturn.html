Saturn

The majestic gas giant known for its iconic rings

Overview

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our solar system. Best known for its magnificent ring system, Saturn is a gas giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~1.43 billion km
  • Diameter: 120,536 km
  • Surface Gravity: ~1.07 g
  • Day Length: ~10.7 hours
  • Year Length: ~29.5 Earth years
  • Rings: 7 main rings and thousands of ringlets
  • Moons: 80+ known moons

Atmosphere & Composition

Saturn’s atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, with layers of ammonia ice, water vapor, and complex organic molecules. Storm systems and powerful winds reach speeds of over 1,800 km/h.

Rings & Moons

Saturn’s rings are composed of ice particles, dust, and rock fragments. They are the most extensive and vivid ring system in the solar system. Titan, its largest moon, has a thick atmosphere and hydrocarbon lakes.

Exploration

The Cassini-Huygens mission (2004–2017) revealed Saturn's complex ring structure, diverse moons, and atmospheric dynamics. The Huygens probe landed on Titan, offering the first surface images of the moon.

Why It’s Unique

Saturn is the least dense planet—it would float in water. Its immense ring system and atmospheric patterns make it a favorite for astronomers and space enthusiasts.
