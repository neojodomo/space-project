Mercury

The smallest planet and closest to the Sun

Overview

Mercury is the innermost planet, orbiting the Sun every ~88 Earth days. With a surface resembling our Moon—cratered and dark—it experiences extremes: searing heat in sunlight and freezing cold in shadow.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~57.9 million km
  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Surface Gravity: ~0.38 g
  • Day Length: ~59 Earth days
  • Year Length: 88 Earth days
  • Temperature: −173 °C to 427 °C
  • No atmosphere: Thin exosphere

Structure & Composition

Mercury's core accounts for most of its mass—rich in iron and nickel. Above lies a silicate mantle and crust. Without a thick atmosphere, impacts from meteoroids directly alter its barren surface.

Orbital Characteristics

Its highly elliptical orbit leads to significant variations in solar radiation. Mercury is tidally locked—completing three rotations for every two orbits around the Sun, causing long days and nights.

Exploration History

Mariner 10 (1974–75) flew by Mercury three times, mapping about 45% of its surface. NASA’s MESSENGER orbited Mercury between 2011–2015, mapping its entire surface and revealing details about its geology and magnetic field.

Why It’s Unique

Mercury has the largest iron core relative to size and experiences one of the most extreme temperature swings in the solar system. It also has water ice deposits hidden in permanently shadowed craters.
