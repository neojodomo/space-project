Jupiter

The Gas Giant with a Massive Storm System

Overview

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is known for its Great Red Spot, a centuries-old storm, and its powerful magnetic field.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~778.5 million km
  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Surface Gravity: ~2.53 g
  • Day Length: ~9.9 hours
  • Year Length: 11.86 Earth years
  • Temperature: −145°C (cloud tops)
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen and Helium

Structure & Composition

Jupiter likely has a rocky core surrounded by metallic hydrogen and an outer layer of molecular hydrogen. It has no solid surface.

Orbit & Moons

Jupiter orbits the Sun at a slower rate due to its massive distance and has dozens of moons, including the four large Galilean moons.

Exploration

Explored by Pioneer, Voyager, Galileo, Juno, and other missions. Its moons, especially Europa, are of great scientific interest.

Unique Features

Jupiter emits more heat than it receives from the Sun and has the most intense radiation belts in the solar system.
