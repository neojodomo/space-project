Venus

The hottest planet and Earth’s twisted twin

Overview

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and similar in size and structure to Earth—but with a hellish atmosphere. It's perpetually shrouded in thick clouds of sulfuric acid and experiences surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~108 million km
  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Surface Gravity: ~0.9 g
  • Day Length: 243 Earth days (retrograde)
  • Year Length: 225 Earth days
  • Temperature: ~465 °C average
  • Atmosphere: Thick CO₂ with sulfuric acid clouds

Structure & Composition

Venus has a metallic core surrounded by a rocky mantle and crust. Its dense atmosphere traps heat via runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system despite being second from the Sun.

Orbital Characteristics

Venus has a nearly circular orbit and rotates very slowly in the opposite direction of most planets. A Venusian day is longer than its year.

Exploration History

The Soviet Union’s Venera missions were the first to land on Venus, with Venera 7 transmitting data from the surface. NASA’s Magellan orbited Venus and mapped 98% of its surface using radar in the early 1990s.

Why It’s Unique

Venus is Earth's closest planetary sibling in terms of size and composition but has evolved in a drastically different way. Its super-rotating atmosphere and extreme volcanic surface make it a haunting example of a planet gone wrong.
