Earth

Our home planet – vibrant, life-sustaining, and unique

Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to support life. Its dynamic systems, vast oceans, and protective atmosphere make it a haven for biodiversity and human civilization.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~149.6 million km
  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Surface Gravity: 1 g
  • Day Length: 24 hours
  • Year Length: 365.25 days
  • Temperature: −88 °C to 58 °C
  • Atmosphere: Nitrogen, Oxygen-rich

Structure & Composition

Earth has a layered structure: solid inner core, liquid outer core, mantle, and crust. Its surface is 71% covered by water. Tectonic plates move continents and generate earthquakes and volcanic activity.

Atmosphere & Climate

Earth’s atmosphere shields it from harmful solar radiation and retains heat through the greenhouse effect. It supports complex weather systems, seasons, and a habitable climate range for life.

Why It’s Unique

Earth is the only planet where liquid water is stable on the surface and where life has evolved. Its magnetic field protects from solar winds, and the Moon stabilizes its axial tilt, moderating climate.
