Neptune

The Windiest Planet in the Solar System

Overview

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun. It is a gas giant and has a deep blue appearance due to methane in its atmosphere. Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system and experiences dynamic, stormy weather patterns.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~4.5 billion km
  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Surface Gravity: ~1.14 g
  • Day Length: ~16 hours
  • Year Length: 165 Earth years
  • Temperature: ~−214 °C
  • Moons: 14 (including Triton)

Atmosphere & Climate

Neptune’s atmosphere consists mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane. The planet is home to supersonic winds exceeding 2,000 km/h and large storm systems like the Great Dark Spot observed by Voyager 2.

Magnetic Field

Neptune’s magnetic field is tilted and offset from its rotation axis, suggesting unusual internal dynamics. It's generated by movements in an electrically conductive layer beneath its icy mantle.

Exploration History

Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to visit Neptune, flying by in 1989. It captured stunning images, discovered new moons, and observed dynamic weather systems and rings surrounding the planet.

Interesting Features

Neptune’s largest moon, Triton, orbits in the opposite direction of the planet’s rotation—a sign it may have been captured. The planet also emits more heat than it receives from the Sun, contributing to its turbulent atmosphere.
